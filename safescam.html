How to protect yourself

What is a protect yourself from scam

  • Be skeptical: If something seems too good to be true, it probably is. Always verify information before trusting it.
  • Don't share personal information: Never share personal information like your social security number, bank account details, or credit card number unless you're sure the person or organization you're dealing with is legitimate.
  • Use secure and trusted websites: When shopping or banking online, make sure the website is secure (the URL should start with "https://") and is a trusted, well-known site.
  • Be careful with emails and messages: Don't click on links in emails or messages from people you don't know. These could lead to scam websites or download harmful software onto your computer.
  • Keep your computer and mobile devices secure: Use antivirus software, keep your operating system up to date, and secure your internet connection.
  • Report scams: If you come across a scam, report it to your local law enforcement agency and to the platform (like your email provider or social media site) where you encountered it.
